# Analysis Playbook

## Market Snapshot

- Identify the variety, exchange, likely main contract, session status, latest price, daily change, volume, and open interest when available.
- Compare price movement with volume/open-interest movement. Rising price with rising open interest is different from a short-covering rebound.

## Technical Role

- Use recent daily bars when available.
- Report trend, support, resistance, volatility state, and whether price is above or below short/medium moving averages.
- Prefer zones over false precision when data quality is partial.

## Fundamentals Role

- For industrial commodities, look for inventory, warehouse receipts, basis, production/consumption headlines, and policy changes.
- For agricultural commodities, look for supply, import, weather, crushing/processing margins, and seasonal demand.
- For financial futures, look for index trend, rates/liquidity, policy, and risk appetite.

## Flow And Positioning Role

- Use open interest, volume, exchange ranking, and main-seat flows when available.
- State whether money flow confirms, diverges from, or does not inform the price move.

## News And Macro Role

- Use only current, source-backed news. Separate verified exchange announcements from market commentary.
- Summarize the catalyst and affected pathway: demand, supply, cost, liquidity, policy, sentiment, or delivery.

## Synthesis

- Finish with a directional bias, confidence level, key invalidation, and what would change the view.
- For trade plans, include trigger, stop, target/management, position sizing caution, and event risk.
- If price is below MA20 but closes positive near support, call it a weak-structure repair unless volume/open-interest and fundamentals confirm a reversal.
- If the snapshot has only quote/technical data, keep confidence at medium-low or lower until source-backed fundamentals and news are added.
- Keep advice framed as research assistance, not personalized investment advice.
